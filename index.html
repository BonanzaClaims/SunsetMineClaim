Sunset Mine Quick Facts



Summary of Claim

Up for sale is the Relief Mine located in Linn County, Oregon. The Relief Mine is a single unpatented claim located on the south side of White Bull mountain, 34 miles from the town of Sweet Home, OR. The Relief Mine produced primarily gold with small amounts of silver, lead and zinc as byproducts. The mine is located on the adjacent side of the mountain where the famous gold rush town of Quartzville was situated. The Relief Mine was driven into the same mountain as Quartzville's most productive mine, the Lawler Mine.

On the claim are four known adits. Two of these adits are on the lower vein with roughly 40 feet of vertical distance between them. Assays taken from the lower vein ran as high as 5.02 oz/ton gold. The third adit is located higher up on the mountain and taps a different vein. The fourth adit is a small prospect high above the first and second adits. There are dozens of open cuts and prospect pits on the property as well.

History

There were two distinct phases of mining history on this property. The first phase occurred during the 1890s when gold was first discovered at the Relief Mine by earlier pioneer miners. These miners discovered and developed the uppermost vein on the property, upon which the upper adit is driven. The second phase of history began with the discovery of the lower vein, which yielded incredibly rich assay values (see table below). This vein was not discovered until the 1930s when a group of mining brothers moved to the area. This vein was not discovered early because it is a “blind vein,” meaning that it does not show on the surface. In fact, it’s amazing it was even discovered at all. It also makes one wonder, how many other gold-rich blind veins are on the claim? It’s up to you to find out.

The Aho brothers came from a family of miners and had a vast knowledge of gold mining. The brothers owned and operated the Relief Mine from the mid 1930s until the 1970s. Because of the mine’s richness, the Aho brothers were able to make a substantial portion of their living from the gold they recovered at the mine.

The hardworking Aho brothers accumulated a large stockpile of gold from the Relief Mine. Several reports state that visitors of the Aho brothers were shown “jars of gold,” another indication of the mine’s richness. The Aho brothers lived on the claim in a small cabin just down the hill from the mine. The ore was processed in a mill that the brothers constructed. Some remnants of the cabin and mill still remain onsite to this day.

Why is there still so much rich gold ore left at this mine? The answer, unfortunately, is tragic. Today, the face of the east drift in the lower adit is obscured by the muck pile from the final blast the Aho brothers set off. According to official reports, a stick of dynamite had not detonated in the previous blast and went off when they returned to clean out the muck pile, instantly killing all three of them. We can only imagine how huge this mine would have been if they had been able to continue mining. It would likely have had large stopes and produced immense amounts of gold.

Lower Adit Results

The lower adit averaged 3.63 oz/ton gold with a maximum value of 5.14 oz/ton. These are absolutely phenomenal values, hands down the best assay results we have ever received and likely some of the highest values ever found in a mine offered for sale on Ebay. The vein averages under 2 inches in width along the 70 feet we sampled. The ore is in a clay gouge, which can be mined without having to remove any waste rock. Another huge benefit of this deposit is that the ore does not need to be crushed. The gold is completely free in the clay gouge and only needs to be screened and milled using gravity methods, this process can be seen in the video below.

Middle Adit Results

One sample was taken from the middle adit, this sample assayed at 0.486 oz/ton gold. There is good potential for gold recovery in this adit. The vein is up to 2 feet and the adit continues for over 40ft. More exploration should be done here and on the surface above the workings.

Upper Adit Results

One sample was taken from the upper adit, this sample assayed at 0.18 oz/ton gold. This adit explores a completely separate vein from the one tapped by the lower two adits. The vein is much wider but contains significantly lower gold values as indicated by the assay above. There are some massive open cuts and trenches on the hillside above this adit as well. This would be a great place to prospect.

Accessibility

The mine can be reached by driving 34 miles from Sweet Home, OR on a paved road. After parking your vehicle, a short, ¼ mile hike is required to reach the entrance of the main adit. Hiking to the main adit takes approximately 15 minutes and is rated moderate. Hiking from the main adit to the upper adit is rated strenuous.
